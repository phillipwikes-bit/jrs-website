SECTION 07  |  WHAT JRS™ CANNOT DO ALONE

What JRS™ Cannot Do on Its Own

JRS™ works. It also has real limits. These are the five places where the standard on its own is not sufficient. Understanding them is part of deploying it with integrity.

01  |  COMPLIANCE FATIGUE
The greatest threat to any manual control is habit.
When a manager starts asking AI to rewrite a record so it meets JRS™ standards, the AI will produce language that looks defensible even when the evidence behind it does not exist. The record looks clean. The file is empty. That is not compliance. It is a more professionally worded liability.
Fix: Before saving, confirm the evidence the record points to actually exists in the file. If it does not, the language fails the standard regardless of how well it reads.
02  |  PROMPT INTEGRITY
JRS™ addresses what gets written. It does not address what drove the writing.
If a manager asks AI to find reasons this person is not a culture fit, and the AI returns a polished justification, the record may look clean. The prompt history tells a different story. Attorneys are already requesting AI chat histories in discovery.
Fix: A manager should be able to state the basis for the decision without the AI's help. If the justification only exists because the AI produced it, the record has not passed the standard.
03  |  SHADOW AI
Many managers never use an official system to draft.
A significant number of managers write feedback in a personal ChatGPT or Claude account and paste it into the official platform. The drafting is the creation. Not the pasting. Any written text that could surface in litigation is a record.
Fix: The standard applies the moment someone starts writing about another person's employment. Say that explicitly. Do not assume managers will figure it out on their own.
04  |  QUALITATIVE OVER-CORRECTION
Observable does not mean a number on a spreadsheet.
Real leadership failures, poor judgment, and harmful conduct are all documentable under JRS™. The question is whether you can describe a specific moment that actually happened.
Fix: Document what happened. When it happened. Who observed it. What was said or done. That is observable. That holds up.
05  |  SAFE HARBOR LIMITATIONS
JRS™ is evidence of reasonable care. It is not a complete shield.
Courts are still working out what adequate human oversight looks like for AI-assisted employment decisions. JRS™ puts the organization in a meaningfully stronger position than one that has nothing. For decisions as significant as a termination, it works best as part of a broader process that includes more than one reviewer and a documented evidence file.
Fix: Treat JRS™ as evidence of good faith, not a substitute for sound process. Work with employment counsel to understand how it interacts with the regulations specific to your decisions.

Apply the standard with discipline. Verify that the evidence it references actually exists. Audit the reasoning behind it. That is what defensible by design actually means.

SECTION 08  |  COMMON QUESTIONS

Common Questions

Different stakeholders have different questions. Use the tabs to find the ones most relevant to you.

How long does it actually take to apply JRS™ to one record? +
Five minutes or less on a well-drafted record. Longer on a record that has problems, because it requires revision before saving. The time investment is front-loaded. Records that pass the check the first time do not require rework later.
Does applying JRS™ mean rewriting every record? +
Not necessarily. Records that are already grounded in observable facts and can stand on their own will pass the check quickly. The standard surfaces problems that are already there. The revision only happens when a problem is found, which is the point.
How do we roll this out to managers without a training program? +
The $12 standard includes a manager guide. Most teams start by sharing the phrase risk reference table and asking managers to check their next record before saving. That is the whole rollout. It does not require an LMS, a training schedule, or a vendor engagement.
We already have a documentation policy. How does JRS™ relate to that? +
JRS™ is a pre-submission check, not a policy. It does not replace your existing documentation policy. It is applied before any record governed by that policy is saved. Think of it as a final quality check at the moment of creation, not a competing framework.
Does this protect against all discrimination claims? +
No. JRS™ is an operational documentation standard. It supports a good faith compliance posture. It does not guarantee protection from employment claims, regulatory action, or litigation. Organizations should consult qualified employment counsel. Applying JRS™ puts your organization in a stronger position than one that has nothing, but it is not a complete shield.

JRS™ is a management standard applied by managers, not legal counsel. It does not create or implicate attorney-client privilege.
